About Sense bot

A general overview of what SenseBot does and how it does it.

Concept

In a world where nearly everyone has access to algorithmic trading technologies but it is only used by Wall Street brokers, we're trying to make learning and experimenting more accessible.

Simple

Sense bot is designed to look more like your favorite social media platform than your typical brokerage site. We try to make navigation easy for people of all backgrounds so that users feel empowered, not overwhelmed.

Transparent

No degree in finance? No problem! At Sense we try to provide information in terms that everyone can understand. The core concepts to our platform are not that difficult, so we want to eliminate the tricky syntax that prevents people without a business degree from understanding them.

Fun

Playing with money is fun, especially when you aren't risking your life savings. By simplifying the experience, we're making it fun to test out your ideas and grow your financial literacy.

Uses

SenseBot originated as a test-mechanism for low-cap stocks, but the possibilities don't end there!

Building models

With nearly instant feedback, you can quickly adjust your models to iron out problems and optimize profits.

Comparing stocks

A model that fails for one stock may be a raging success for another. Quickly apply models to different stocks to explore different performance across entire industries.

Find help

Post to the boards to see if others have ideas that can take your model to the next level.

Limitations

As with any financial analysis tool, SenseBot isn't perfect. Calculations are based on educated estimates of bid/ask availability. As a result, we cannot predict exactly how the stock would have performed, but we can get pretty close!

Due to the limitations of publicly accessible stock market data, Sense bot is not able to execute trades based on actual transaction, Level I, & Level II data. Historical stock availability is estimated based on volume over a given interval and does not take into account coinciding Bid/Ask sizes.

Just because a model yielded good results in the past does not mean that it will preform the same in the future. Many factors contribute to stock price and performance, some of which are completely unpredictable.
